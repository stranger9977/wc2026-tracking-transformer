Why this matters for 2026

FIFA already speaks the language of space

FIFA's Enhanced Football Intelligence — debuted in 2022, carried into 2026 — names the off-ball game directly. PFF's human grades let us operationalize and validate that vocabulary today.

  • Line breaks — moving the ball "beyond the deepest player in an opposition unit" (through, around, or over).
  • Receptions behind midfield & defensive lines — "where players receive the ball in relation to the opposition's team shape."
  • Pressure on the ball — "closing the space between themselves and the player on the ball… limits the time a player has."
  • Team shape & defensive line height — positional structure and "how deep a team defends… how high it pushes."
  • Forced turnovers & phases of play — space denied, sliced by build-up / progression / final third.

FIFA's metrics are algorithm-derived from tracking. PFF's are human judgments of off-ball quality — the ground truth those algorithms approximate. That's our edge.

How to read this deck

What we computed — and the honest caveats

Space creators

Events PFF tagged createsSpace, attributed to the on-ball actor who played the ball that created space (1,422 tags).

Got open / ignored

betterOptionPlayerId — the open teammate a passer failed to find (518 tags). A "getting open" signal.

Under pressure

Actions with pressureType ∈ {P, A, L} — the pressure annotation OBSO's author said the model needs.

Movement / position grades

Mostly deduction metrics (movement ~71% negative, position ~99%): "best" = least-bad. Read with care.

Caveat carried everywhere: PFF tracking is broadcast-derived — off-ball players go off-camera, and tags are logged only on analyst-selected events. Counts are conservative and biased toward on-camera, high-leverage moments. Exploratory, not exhaustive.

Players · Space created

Who creates space

Total createsSpace actions, full tournament. The literal off-ball leaderboard.

Messi leads the World Cup in space creation (25) — ahead of France's wide pair, Dembélé and Mbappé. Morocco's Ziyech and Ounahi crack the top five despite their seeding: the semi-final run was no fluke off the ball.

Players · Space created under pressure

Space, with a defender in your face

Space-creating actions logged while under pressure (pressureType ∈ P/A/L). The hard version of the skill.

23 of Messi's 25 space-creations came under pressure — the highest pressured total at the tournament. This is the quantified version of "Messi walks, then the game opens."

Players · Space created per match (rate)

The per-90 view — who punched above their minutes

Space-creations ÷ matches appeared (min. 5 total, 2+ matches). Rewards short, explosive runs.

Hirving Lozano created space 5.7×/match — the tournament's top rate — on a Mexico side that went home in the group stage. Pedri (3.5) is the only deep-runner here from a team that advanced, a 2026 name to watch.

Players · Got open & ignored

The most-missed open men

Times a player was flagged betterOptionPlayerId — they got open, and the pass never came.

Morocco's En-Nesyri was the single most-ignored open man (8×). Three Germans (Raum, Gnabry, Müller) crowd the board — a quantified signature of the chance-creation that went to waste in a group-stage exit.

Players · Volume under pressure

The engines: most actions under pressure

Total on-ball actions performed while pressed — the players their teams trusted in tight space.

Messi (472) again leads, with Croatia's midfield trio (Modrić, Kovačić, Brozović) and Argentina's De Paul close behind. Pedri's 69.5 pressured actions/match is the highest rate on the board — Spain ran everything through him.

Teams · Space created

Which teams manufactured the most space

Total createsSpace actions by squad.

Croatia (97) and Morocco (89) top the chart — two midfield-driven sides that overachieved their seeding, ahead of champions Argentina and France. Space creation, not star power, defined the surprise semi-finalists.

Teams · Actions under pressure

Who lived in the tight areas

Total actions performed under pressure, by squad.

Argentina (3,001) absorbed and generated the most pressured play of any team, with finalists France and the two semi-finalists right behind. The deepest runs in Qatar were the ones most comfortable with a defender attached.

Teams · Open men ignored

Space created — but not found

Total betterOption events by squad: open teammates the passer missed.

A double-edged stat: high totals mean a team generated openings (Argentina, Portugal) — but also left value on the table. The connection between movers and passers is itself a 2026 scouting axis.

Players · Off-ball grades — read carefully

The "least-bad" movers

PFF's movementGrade is logged almost entirely as a penalty for poor off-ball runs (~71% negative). So the top of this list = players the analysts dinged least, not "best mover" awards. Small samples — directional only.

    Only 368 movement-graded and 1,300 position-graded events exist tournament-wide — too sparse to crown anyone. The signal is in who gets repeatedly flagged (a B2-style validation target), not in a leaderboard. Honest framing beats a misleading ranking.

    The story so far

    Eight things the data says about space

    • Messi is the tournament's space king — #1 in space created (25), space created under pressure (23), and total pressured actions (472).
    • France's threat is its width — Dembélé and Mbappé rank #2 and #3 in space created.
    • Morocco & Croatia overachieved off the ball — the two surprise semi-finalists led all teams in space created.
    • Lozano's 5.7 space-creations/match was the top rate — on a team eliminated in the group.
    • Germany's exit, quantified — three Germans among the most-ignored open men.
    • En-Nesyri got open and was missed 8 times — the most of anyone.
    • Pedri is the 2026 bridge — top pressured-actions rate (69.5/match) and a deep-runner who advanced.
    • Champions thrive in tight space — Argentina led all teams in pressured actions and openings generated.

Chemistry leaderboards

Two ways of measuring chemistry, in one place. The event-based leaderboard ranks pairs by joint on-ball impact (JOI / JDI, built on VAEP); the attention-based leaderboard ranks pairs the tracking transformer watches together off the ball. The disagreement view surfaces pairs where the two lenses tell very different stories.

Distribution, visible pairs
One bar per pair, ranked by the active metric.

JOI90 = joint offensive impact per 90 minutes shared (formal definition). JDI90 = joint defensive impact per 90 minutes shared (formal definition). Both built on VAEP — P(score) − P(concede) credit per action.

Every pair the transformer attended to most across the 44 WC22 matches with tracking. Offensive = both forward of midfield. Defensive = both defenders or GK. Cross = one of each. Lift normalises against the team's median pair, so 2× means "twice the attention of a baseline pair from the same squad."

GKs dominate raw attention because they touch the ball every defensive sequence; hidden by default.

How to read this

For every pair present in both datasets, we compute its percentile rank on JOI90 (event-based) and on attention lift (transformer-based), then score the gap as |pct(JOI) − pct(attention)|. The top rows are pairs the model watches together but rarely connect on the ball, or vice versa — the off-ball vs on-ball gap. GK pairs are excluded so the ranking isn't dominated by the goalkeeper's universal touch.

The headline: Wales' Ethan Ampadu + Joe Allen (both CM) rank p100 on attention lift but only p18 on JOI — the transformer watches them constantly but they barely combine on the ball. That's the cleanest off-ball-only chemistry signal in the dataset. The Welsh double-pivot shaped the build-up without touching it.

Percentiles are computed inside the dataset that produced each metric, then joined by team + unordered player-pair. A pair only appears here if it has a non-zero attention record and survives the event-based minimum minutes filter on this page.

PFF event + tracking, 64 matches (44 with tracking), FIFA World Cup 2022. Bransen & Van Haaren (Sloan 2020) + a transformer encoder over 22-player tracking.

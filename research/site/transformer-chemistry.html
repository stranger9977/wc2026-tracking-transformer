Transformer Chemistry: the off-ball view

The JOI / JDI leaderboards see only on-ball events. This tab is the off-ball complement: a tracking transformer watches all 22 players + ball at 5 Hz and tells us which pairs it actually watches together — chemistry the event data can't see. Does it lift VAEP, and which pairs come out on top?

Attention chemistry leaderboard

Every pair the transformer attended to most across the 44 WC22 matches with tracking, grouped by what kind of partnership it is. Offensive = both players forward of midfield. Defensive = both players defenders or GK. Cross = one off, one def — the lines that connect the two halves of the team. The same three colours show up on the team maps below.

GKs dominate raw attention because they touch the ball every defensive sequence; hidden by default.

Lift normalizes attention against each team's median pair — so the ranking surfaces partnerships the model watches together more than its typical pair from that team, not just pairs of players who happen to log a lot of minutes. 2× means "twice the attention of a baseline pair from the same squad." Triples and quads use the same scaling: average pair-attention inside the group, divided by the team baseline.

Frame-level model performance

How to read this

AUC is the chance a random scoring frame ranks above a random non-scoring frame — 0.5 is a coin flip, 1.0 is perfect. The event-only baseline (on actions, not frames) is score AUC 0.681, concede AUC 0.671. The tracking transformer's frame-level heads reach val_auc_score ≈ 0.81 and val_auc_concede ≈ 0.82 at the best epoch on the held-out 8-match split — not directly comparable to the event-level number (different prediction unit, different validation set) but within the same family of metrics and substantially above chance.

Frame-level metrics not yet exported. The training script writes output/training_metrics_frame_vaep.json; an export step copies it into data/vaep_metrics_transformer.json for this page.

Method

The JOI / JDI metrics on the other tabs use event-level VAEP — one number per on-ball action. That throws away ~95 % of the game: every off-ball run, every defensive shape adjustment, every moment where a player pulls a defender out of position without touching the ball. This tab adds a tracking transformer — a model that sees all 22 players plus ball at 5 Hz and emits two per-frame probabilities (a frame-level lift of the same VAEP framework) plus an attention map showing which players the model is conditioning on. We use that for (1) per-frame chemistry traces in the Interactive Plays tab and (2) attention chemistry networks — sum the transformer's pair attention across the tournament for a chemistry view that catches off-ball signal that JOI/JDI can't see.

The backbone is a transformer encoder over a 23-token state (22 players + 1 ball), each token a 7-feature vector. Two BCE classification heads sit on top of the shared backbone: P(team in possession scores within 10 s) and P(team in possession concedes within 10 s). Targets are derived from PFF goal events; training uses a 36-match train / 8-match val split with class-weighted BCE. Backbone + training script live at src/wc2026_tracking_transformer/. Best-epoch validation: score AUC ≈ 0.81, concede AUC ≈ 0.82.

· Parked · Self-Made Space

Who turns movement into a passing lane.

For every attacking player without the ball, how much receiving space do they hold open — and how much of it did their own run create? Space-native square metres of controllable pitch; ball-carrier and keepers excluded.

why parked The team xG receipt is null and not significant (ρ=−0.26, bootstrap CI [−0.63, 0.44] spans zero, n=11 teams; 8 of 11 teams contribute a single sampled match). Read it as "no relationship," not anti-correlation. The hero clip also needs reframing before this goes back on the main page.

Subtract a velocity-frozen baseline and you isolate space a player's own run created. The board reshuffles fullbacks-in-acres down and active runners — Correa, Ziyech, Mitoma — up. Their CIs broadly overlap, so this is a top cluster, not a single leader.

So what? — the xG receipt (null — why this is parked)

Caveats & honesty (read me)
  • xG receipt is noisy and NOT significant: ρ=−0.26, bootstrap CI [−0.63, 0.44] spans zero (n=11 teams). 8 of 11 teams contribute only 1 sampled match each, so per-team SMS is high-variance — read it as "no relationship," not anti-correlation.
  • Raw SMS rewards open, low-value grass; the leaderboard is fullback/winger-heavy by construction. This is a STYLE/volume signal, not a danger ranking. Turning space into danger is the P-OBSO bridge's job.
  • Occlusion: 36.3% of credited player-frames here were ESTIMATED (PFF, biased to forwards/wingers). The board gates every space claim to VISIBLE frames and flags players tracked majority-by-estimation (est badge).
  • Bounded sample: stride 15 (~2 Hz), coarse 32×22 control grid, regulation periods only, 8 knockout matches, 65,386 frames. Star-heavy, not the full tournament.
  • Possession + ball-carrier are heuristic (nearest-player-to-ball, no event join).
· Parked · Space Above Replacement

Who makes room nobody else would.

Off-ball controllable space minus what a replacement-level player wins in the same pitch zone (space-native m², NOT xT-weighted). It credits openness — and the most open space belongs to deep counter sides spreading into vacated grass, not the congested final thirds where high-xG teams actually work.

why parked The xG receipt is orthogonal — effectively zero (ρ≈0.00, wide CI [−0.59, 0.62], n=11 teams). That orthogonality is an honest finding, but as a standalone "so what" it doesn't carry a page, and the replacement-baseline clip needs a clearer telling before this rejoins the main study.

SAR surface — Messi's footprint vs the replacement baseline

Toggle vs replacement to dissolve his surface toward the flat positional baseline — you literally watch the extra space appear and vanish as he drifts off the ball. Messi's sample SAR is +51.1 m² (rank 58/167, well-tracked); Argentina's central engine (de Paul, Enzo, Mac Allister) runs negative — they work congested midfield while the wide players find the room.

Tie-aware leaderboard — space above replacement

Abde Ezzalzouli is a clean single leader (his CI separates from #2). The board is led by relentless off-ball movers — Morocco's wingers, Japan's fullbacks. Hover to ghost a player's excess footprint onto the pitch.

Team SAR (whole-pitch mean)

Morocco and Japan — deep-block / counter sides — win the most open space and are mid-pack on xG. The high-xG possession teams (Spain, England) run negative SAR: they make their chances in tight space, not open grass. The orthogonality, in one table.

So what? — the xG receipt (orthogonal ≈ 0 — why this is parked)

Caveats & honesty
  • Occlusion: 38.8% of sampled positions are ESTIMATED (biased to forwards/wingers). The board gates on ≥15 VISIBLE frames; high-SAR wingers (Boufal 56%, Mitoma 60%) carry heavy ESTIMATED shares (est badge).
  • Possession/orientation are heuristic (no event join); velocity finite-differenced at ~2 Hz.
  • 8 regulation matches at 2 Hz, star/contender-heavy; the xG receipt has only n=11 teams, so the CI is wide ([−0.59, 0.62]).
  • The replacement baseline (30 zones, global mean 276.3 m²) is computed FROM this sample — SAR is relative to the sampled population, not an absolute league baseline.
  • SAR credits openness and deliberately is NOT a threat metric — the orthogonal-to-xG finding (ρ≈0) is the honest "so what." The threat bridge is P-OBSO, not SAR.

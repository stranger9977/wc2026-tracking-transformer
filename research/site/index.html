What is player chemistry?

Chemistry, in this project, is a measure of how well two players' actions compound on the pitch — how much offensive value they create together and how much defensive value they preserve. The framework comes from Lotte Bransen and Jan Van Haaren's 2020 MIT Sloan paper, Player Chemistry: Striving for a Perfectly Balanced Soccer Team. We reapply it here to the 2022 FIFA Men's World Cup using PFF event data (64 matches, all 32 squads).

Why this matters for a World Cup

Every four years, players who gel weekly at their clubs are asked to play out of sync for their country. Chemistry is one quantitative lens on the gap between club familiarity and national-team novelty: not "who is the best player," but which pairs amplify each other. That framing matters because tournament football rewards combinations more than individual talent — a midfield duo that has played 90 minutes together carries information a roster of stars does not.

Scope of v1: the pair-level JOI / JDI / Team-Builder analyses below are computed entirely within the World Cup itself — they're a self-contained look at which national-team partnerships produced and prevented value in 64 matches. The bigger "did the player look worse than at his club" question requires the same player's club season — see "Next step" further down.

The two metrics

JOI Joint Offensive Impact

The sum of VAEP action-values along consecutive same-team interactions between two players. If P passes to Q and Q's next action is a shot, both VAEP values contribute to JOI(P, Q).

\( \mathrm{JOI}(p, q) = \sum_{(a_i, a_{i+1}) \in S_{p,q}} V(a_i) + V(a_{i+1}) \)

Normalized per 90 minutes shared on the pitch (JOI90) to compare pairs with different playing time.

JDI Joint Defensive Impact

For every opponent \(o\) a pair faces, JDI credits the pair when \(o\)'s actual offensive impact comes in below expectation. The credit is split among same-team pairs by their responsibility share \(R(p,q,o)\), derived from the 5×5 position grid (closer to the threat → larger share).

\( \mathrm{JDI}_m(p, q) = \sum_o \big( \mathbb{E}[\mathrm{OI}_o] - \mathrm{OI}_o \big) \cdot R_m(p, q, o) \cdot \tfrac{\mathrm{mins}_m(p,q,o)}{90} \)

\( R_m(p,q,o) = \tfrac{1}{2}\big(r_p(o) + r_q(o)\big) \) and \( r_p(o) \propto 1/d(p,o) \) in the (mirrored) 5×5 grid. Aggregates per 90 minutes shared on the pitch (JDI90).

The 5×5 responsibility grid

Defensive responsibility is split into 25 zones (5 horizontal × 5 vertical bands of the pitch). For each zone, we compute each player's share of defensive actions while they were on the pitch. JDI rewards pairs that jointly suppress more opponent threat than expected in zones where both players are responsible.

Tournament stories: chemistry on the pitch

Ten compact reads, one per knockout side: top JOI pair (offensive partnership) and top JDI pair (defensive partnership), plus one sentence on what the metric is actually picking up. These are the stories the JOI / JDI tables turn into when you cross-reference them with the games.

Form at the tournament: who out-played the positional baseline?

For each player we compare their per-90 offensive impact (OI) at WC22 to an expected per-90 computed only from this dataset. Because most players appear in just 2–4 matches, the expected per-90 is heavily shrunk toward a positional prior (the average OI/90 of GK / DEF / MID / FWD players in the corpus). Δ OI/90 > 0 means a player outran what a typical player of their position produced at the tournament.

⚠ What this is and isn't

With only WC22 data this is not a club-vs-national comparison. It's a within-tournament gap against a positional baseline — useful for flagging surprise performances, but it can't directly answer "did this player look worse than at his club." For that we need their club season too.

Next step: join the open StatsBomb event corpus (some 2021–22 Premier League / La Liga / Champions League seasons) on player_id, recompute each player's VAEP OI/90 across their club matches, and compare to their WC22 OI/90. That delta is the real chemistry-friction signal. The pipeline here is structured so it slots in: see research/src/chemistry/loaders/ for the loader pattern.

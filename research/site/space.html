Space.
Soccer's dark matter.

0 Where the danger lives

Before space, the value of space.

Not all grass is worth the same. Expected Threat (xT) — Karun Singh's canonical model — asks a simple question of every spot on the pitch: if the ball arrived here, how much closer to a goal does the team get? It is the value layer the space metrics will be measured against — and it is its own story, not a multiplier woven into every number.

The xT surface — attacking left → right, opponent goal at the right

low valuehigh value

The danger isn't at the goal line — it's the central pocket just outside the box (peak xT ≈ 0.257). That is where a received ball is worth the most. Everything wide and deep is low-value grass. Hold that picture: most of the “space” the next four metrics measure is, by design, not high-xT space — which is exactly why each one needs its own honesty test.

xT meets space only once, deliberately, at the P-OBSO bridge (Act 3). Everywhere else the space metrics are space-native — square metres of controllable pitch, not threat in disguise.

1 Self-Made Space

Who turns movement into a passing lane.

For every attacking player without the ball, how much receiving space do they hold open — and how much of it did their own run create? Space-native square metres of controllable pitch; ball-carrier and keepers excluded. So what? — the xG receipt

Caveats & honesty (read me)
  • xG receipt is noisy and NOT significant: ρ=−0.26, bootstrap CI [−0.63, 0.44] spans zero (n=11 teams). 8 of 11 teams contribute only 1 sampled match each, so per-team SMS is high-variance — read it as “no relationship,” not anti-correlation.
  • Raw SMS rewards open, low-value grass; the leaderboard is fullback/winger-heavy by construction. This is a STYLE/volume signal, not a danger ranking. Turning space into danger is the P-OBSO bridge's job.
  • Occlusion: 36.3% of credited player-frames here were ESTIMATED (PFF, biased to forwards/wingers). The board gates every space claim to VISIBLE frames and flags players tracked majority-by-estimation (est badge).
  • Bounded sample: stride 15 (~2 Hz), coarse 32×22 control grid, regulation periods only, 8 knockout matches, 65,386 frames. Star-heavy, not the full tournament.
  • Possession + ball-carrier are heuristic (nearest-player-to-ball, no event join).
2 Defensive Gravity

Who bends the back line.

Pure space, no goals: how many markers an attacker ties up (drawn), amplified by how hard those defenders sprint at him (chase-pull). Target men and focal attackers warp the most; roaming creators like Messi score lower because they slip between markers instead of pinning them. The surface is the defender pitch-control field — watch the block collapse toward the gravity attacker and a lane tear open.

Pedro is a genuine clean #1 (his CI clears the field). Below him the rank-2 tier is a real cluster — Ronaldo (the most chase-pull, but 73% ESTIMATED), Weghorst, Amallah, En-Nesyri, El Yamiq. Each row shows its two components: drawn markers (“I occupy bodies”) vs chase-pull m/s (“I make them sprint”). Hover to pin a player onto the surface.

Team gravity

So what? — the xG receipt

Caveats & honesty
  • Space-native by construction: gravity is geometry (drawn markers × inward defender chase), never xT- or goal-weighted. The engine is used only for the hero defender-control surface.
  • xG correlation is team-level over n=12 teams with a 90% CI that crosses zero — directional, correlation not causation.
  • 10 star-heavy/knockout matches at ~2 Hz; opponent and game-state shape every number.
  • ~40.9% of PFF positions are ESTIMATED (biased to forwards/wingers); 45 of 182 rows are occlusion-flagged (est badge), including Ronaldo 73% and En-Nesyri 59%.
  • Velocity (hence chase-pull) is finite-differenced at ~2 Hz; sub-second closing bursts are averaged out.
  • Tie-aware: rows whose 90% CI overlaps the rank-group leader share a rank — no false single #1.
3 P-OBSO · the bridge

Who owns the dangerous space when the ball isn't at their feet.

This is the one metric that is not space-native — the deliberate bridge: pitch control × Expected Threat. It scores the off-ball runner who controls the most valuable real estate the ball could profitably reach. Scrub Mbappé's run in the Argentina–France final: a bright danger-pocket blooms ahead of the run, before the pass exists.

Off-ball danger controllers — with a small-sample flag

Honest correction surfaced: several headline names (Lautaro, Dybala, Correa, Rashford) are cameo substitutes with <4 min sampled, effectively one match — their CIs can't see between-match variance. We split them into a clearly-labelled small-sample tier so they don't pose as tournament-leading controllers; the substantial-minutes group (En-Nesyri, Mbappé, Petković, Messi) is the trustworthy read.

So what? — the xG receipt (the strong one)

Caveats & honesty
  • The team xG receipt depends on excluding StatsBomb penalty-shootout shots (period 5, ~0.78 xG each) and matching the tracking sample to regulation (periods 1–2). With shootout xG included the correlation flips to null (ρ=−0.20) — a real bug, caught and fixed. n=10 teams over 8 knockout matches; treat ρ=0.71 as suggestive.
  • P-OBSO stock (mean controlled-danger area/frame) barely discriminates teams (63.7–66.4) and is NOT correlated with xG — by design. The team board ranks the flow (danger-moment rate).
  • Player headline rests on present-frames-only averaging, which favours late-game attacking subs; the small-sample tier flag is the disclosure. 38.7% of positions ESTIMATED; En-Nesyri (72%), Correa (59%), Dybala (52%) flagged.
  • Possession + carrier identity are heuristic (nearest-player-to-ball), not event-joined. Velocity is finite-differenced at ~2 Hz. Extra time excluded from both tracking and xG.
4 Space Above Replacement

Who makes room nobody else would.

Off-ball controllable space minus what a replacement-level player wins in the same pitch zone (space-native m², NOT xT-weighted). It credits openness — and the most open space belongs to deep counter sides spreading into vacated grass, not the congested final thirds where high-xG teams actually work. Scrub Messi's run: his bright footprint over the flat replacement baseline; the excess is his SAR.

SAR surface — Messi's footprint vs the replacement baseline

baselineabove replacement

Toggle vs replacement to dissolve his surface toward the flat positional baseline — you literally watch the extra space appear and vanish as he drifts off the ball. Messi's sample SAR is +51.1 m² (rank 58/167, well-tracked); Argentina's central engine (de Paul, Enzo, Mac Allister) runs negative — they work congested midfield while the wide players find the room.

Tie-aware leaderboard — space above replacement

Abde Ezzalzouli is a clean single leader (his CI separates from #2). The board is led by relentless off-ball movers — Morocco's wingers, Japan's fullbacks. Hover to ghost a player's excess footprint onto the pitch.

Team SAR (whole-pitch mean)

Morocco and Japan — deep-block / counter sides — win the most open space and are mid-pack on xG. The high-xG possession teams (Spain, England) run negative SAR: they make their chances in tight space, not open grass. The orthogonality, in one table.

So what? — the xG receipt (orthogonal, on purpose)

Caveats & honesty
  • Occlusion: 38.8% of sampled positions are ESTIMATED (biased to forwards/wingers). The board gates on ≥15 VISIBLE frames; high-SAR wingers (Boufal 56%, Mitoma 60%) carry heavy ESTIMATED shares (est badge).
  • Possession/orientation are heuristic (no event join); velocity finite-differenced at ~2 Hz.
  • 8 regulation matches at 2 Hz, star/contender-heavy; the xG receipt has only n=11 teams, so the CI is wide ([−0.59, 0.62]).
  • The replacement baseline (30 zones, global mean 276.3 m²) is computed FROM this sample — SAR is relative to the sampled population, not an absolute league baseline.
  • SAR credits openness and deliberately is NOT a threat metric — the orthogonal-to-xG finding (ρ≈0) is the honest “so what.” The threat bridge is P-OBSO, not SAR.

The thesis, live in the host-nation tournament.

The 2022 study had to reconstruct space from tracking and labels because FIFA's 2022 Enhanced Football Intelligence was physical-only (distance, sprints, top speed). The 2026 EFI is rich and live — it publishes a threat number, the closest public cousin to the P-OBSO bridge above. Here are the early WC2026 leaders, pulled straight from the live feed.

I The open man nobody found

The cost of the closed eye.

Every so often a teammate gets open — and the player on the ball just… doesn't see him. PFF analysts tagged 518 of these moments at the 2022 World Cup. Strip out 8 where the player should've shot himself and 2 mis-attributed to the wrong team, and 508 are real ignored open men.

The "most ignored open man" — and why there's no #1

Six players were each ignored 7 times, and their counts are statistically indistinguishable (same Poisson 95% interval). So this is a tie tier, not a ranking — naming a single "most ignored man" would be false precision. The clean fact: nobody got open-and-overlooked more often than this group.

Team totals are exposure-driven (teams played 3–7 matches): Argentina's 32 is the raw tournament-high on the way to the trophy; per game, Germany's rate leads over just 3 matches.

II The half-turn that bent the final

Receiving already pointed at goal.

The most valuable reception in football is the one you take already turned toward goal, behind a line that's just been broken — it converts a line-break into instant threat instead of a sideways recycle. Across 64 matches there were 848 of them: only 18.2% of all line-break receptions arrive facing goal.

Tournament leaders — a 36-player tie, no single #1

Thirty-six players land between 6 and 11 turned-under-line receptions, and every count sits inside the leader's 95% interval — so even the top of the cluster (Kramarić, 11) is not a supportable #1. The honest read: this is the leading cluster of half-turn receivers, not a ranked list.

How we know what we know

One honest caveat. Broadcast tracking only sees what's on camera, so 40.9% of player positions are estimated, not tracked — and the blind spot skews toward forwards and wingers, the very players a space metric wants. That's why the few soft xT reads above are flagged directional rather than precise. But the counts that carry these stories — who got open and was ignored, who received on the turn — come from human labels and aren't affected.

Club vs national: who travels?

This tab cross-references each WC22 player's offensive impact per 90 (OI/90) at the World Cup against their OI/90 in every other competition we have event data for. The framing question: "is the player who under-performs at the tournament also under-performing at their club — or is it a national-team friction effect?"

⚠ What this is — and what's missing

  • What we have. 64 PFF WC22 matches (the JOI/JDI corpus), plus StatsBomb open-data event streams for: WC22 itself (cross-validation), Euro 2024, Copa America 2024, Ligue 1 2022/23 (partial — mostly PSG), and Bundesliga 2023/24 (partial — mostly Bayern). Ligue 1 and Bundesliga in StatsBomb open data are sample seasons, not full ones.
  • What we don't have. Full Premier League, La Liga, or Serie A coverage for 2022/23. Most international stars are missing club-side data. Coverage is best for PSG players (Mbappé, Hakimi, Donnarumma, Vitinha, Marquinhos), Bayern players (Müller, Kimmich, Musiala, Sané, Gnabry), and any WC22 player who also appeared at Euro/Copa 2024.
  • Calibration caveat. WC22 numbers come from our PFF pipeline; other contexts are StatsBomb data passed through the same VAEP model. Action-coverage and tagging conventions differ slightly between providers, so treat the delta as directional, not absolute. The PFF WC22 vs StatsBomb WC22 row pair is the calibration check — if those agree for a player, we trust the other rows too.
  • Minutes proxy. StatsBomb open data doesn't ship lineups in a form we can join in-place for every match, so we estimate on-pitch time from the span of action timestamps per player per match. Over-estimates marginally; consistent across competitions, so the delta is still meaningful.

How we control for the competition gap

What's a z-score, in plain English? Take every forward playing in a competition. Compute their average OI/90 and how spread out the group is (the standard deviation σ). A z-score answers "how many σ above (or below) the average is this player?" A z of 0 means league-average for his role. +1 means "one σ above the typical forward in this competition." −1 means one σ below. The reason we need this: Bundesliga forwards average a lot more OI/90 than tournament forwards, so a +0.5 raw delta might really just be "your league has more OI on the table." Z-scores ask the harder question: relative to the players around you in the same competition, did you stand out more here than there?

Math / Python (technical audience) 
# research/scripts/compute_cross_context.py
def zscore(group_df, col):
    if len(group_df) < 5:           # too few peers to compare against
        return float("nan")
    mu = group_df[col].mean()
    sd = group_df[col].std(ddof=0) or 1.0
    return (group_df[col] - mu) / sd

# We group by (competition, role) and z-score within each cohort.
all_rows["z_oi_per90"] = (
    all_rows.groupby(["competition", "role"], group_keys=False)
    .apply(lambda g: zscore(g, "oi_per90"), include_groups=False)
)
# Δ z = z_other − z_wc22. Strips out league-wide OI inflation.
  • Raw Δ OI/90 — pure difference. Best for storytelling, worst for fairness: a Bundesliga forward averages more OI/90 than a tournament forward, so the raw Δ flatters anyone playing in a high-volume league.
  • Δ z-score (recommended) — within each (competition, role) we compute the OI/90 z-score (player minus the mean of same-role peers, divided by σ). Comparing z-scores across competitions strips out the league-wide scoring environment. A z of +1.5 in Bundesliga vs +0.5 at WC22 means "this player was much further ahead of his peer cohort at the club than at the tournament."
  • Δ OI / touch — switch denominator from minutes to ball touches. Players in possession-heavy teams (PSG, Bayern) inflate per-90 just by having the ball more; per-touch isolates the productivity of each on-ball action.
  • Still uncontrolled. Opponent strength (a Ligue 1 mid-table match is not WC22 quarter-finals) and tactical role (a player can be a 9 at his club but a 10 at his country). Those need team-Elo or per-match opponent ratings — flagged as next step.

Provider calibration: PFF WC22 vs StatsBomb WC22

Same player, same tournament, two different data providers (PFF and StatsBomb both ship event data for WC22). Δ here measures the gap between our PFF pipeline's OI/90 and the StatsBomb-derived OI/90 for the same player at the same tournament. Big calibration deltas mean the two providers tagged the same matches differently — that helps bound how much of any club-vs-national gap is a "real" chemistry effect vs a provider artifact.

Implications for 2026

The chemistry-friction signal is strongest for attackers and creative midfielders — players whose value depends on partnerships. Compare a striker's club OI/90 in his usual league with his national OI/90 at WC22, and you get a directional read on whether his country has assembled the pieces to play him. For 2026 the same player tracked across WC26 qualifying and his 2025/26 club season should give a 6-month look-ahead on who is "carrying chemistry" into the tournament.

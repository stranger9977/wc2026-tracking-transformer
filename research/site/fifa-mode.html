FIFA Mode: paper talent vs. what actually happened

EA Sports publishes a deterministic number for every nation and every player — a weighted average of in-game attributes. It knows nothing about chemistry, form, manager, or who actually starts. That makes it a useful null model.

The gap between paper rating and what actually happened is where chemistry lives. A team that overperforms its paper rank by 4 (Argentina, WC22) is telling us paper talent isn't the whole story. A team that underperforms by 12 (Belgium) is telling us the same thing, in reverse. The whole rest of this site is an attempt to explain that gap — to put a number on the chemistry, the dependency, the depth. FIFA Mode is the prior we're arguing against.

And the non-linearity that matters. A football match plays 11. FIFA's team rating is dominated by those 11. Depth (player 12-23) barely shifts the number, even though depth wins tournaments through fatigue, injury, and rotation. So the rating systematically over-rewards top-loaded squads. France in 2022 had the largest top-11 / 12-23 gap of any major nation: brilliant XI, ordinary depth, ran out of legs in the final.

Where to look for upsets

Apply the WC22 patterns: narrow depth gaps survive tournaments better; midfield-heavy ratings tend to overperform attack-heavy ones (Croatia 2018-2022 was the cleanest example); top-loaded squads underperform when their stars are tired or marked. Look at the depth gap column. Norway 8.x, France 8.2, England 6.0 — the obvious ones to fade if you're betting against paper.

Method & sources

FIFA team ratings are EA Sports' published national-team overall ratings from the relevant game version: FIFA 23 (released 30 Sep 2022) for WC22 and FIFA 26 (released 26 Sep 2025) for WC26. They're a weighted aggregate of the top-11 players' individual Overall ratings. Depth gap is the average rating of the top-11 minus the average of squad members 12-23.

FIFA 23 individual ratings were cross-referenced from dexerto's WC22 player ratings article, fifaratings.com, FUTBIN, and sofifa archives. Matched against the WC22 PFF tracking-data player roster (633 players) by normalized name; ~370 players matched. Players with <150 minutes are excluded from the scatter (sample-size noise dominates).

OI/90 (offensive impact per 90) is the same chemistry-aware metric used on the Club vs National tab — Bransen & Van Haaren's offensive xT-like score derived from event tracking. It accounts for what a player does on the ball; it doesn't see chemistry, off-ball runs, or pressing (that's what the rest of the site is for).

FIFA 26 ratings were scraped from fifaratings.com country pages (sofifa.com was 403-walled this session). For the 12 WC26-likely nations EA did not license, the team-Overall is aggregated from individual player Overalls.

Tournament result rank: winner=1, RU=2, 3rd=3, 4th=4, QF losers ranked 5-8 by group-stage points, R16 losers 9-16, group-stage exits 17-32 by points then goal diff. Standard FIFA tournament ranking.

Next → the Club vs National tab is the players' analogue of the team-level lens, and Top Pairs is where chemistry — the thing FIFA's number ignores — actually shows up.

FIFA Mode reads paper ratings from EA Sports' FIFA 23 (WC22) and FIFA 26 (WC26 prediction); tournament results from FIFA's official records; player OI/90 from this site's tracking data.

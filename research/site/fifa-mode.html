FIFA Mode: paper talent vs. what actually happens

EA Sports publishes a deterministic team rating for every national side. It's a weighted average of the best 11 players' Overall scores — it knows nothing about chemistry, form, manager, or who actually starts. That makes it a useful null model: how far did each team finish from where their paper rating said they should?

The non-linearity. A football match plays 11 players. FIFA's team rating is dominated by those 11. Depth (player 12-23) barely shifts the number, even though depth wins tournaments through fatigue, injury, and rotation. So the rating systematically over-rewards top-loaded squads. France in 2022 had the largest top-11 / 12-23 gap of any major nation: brilliant XI, ordinary depth. They reached the final and ran out of legs in extra time.

The five biggest stories

WC 2026 · FIFA 26 paper-talent ranking

FIFA 26 was released 26 Sep 2025. WC26 hasn't happened — these are the ratings as of launch. Treat them as paper rankings, not predictions. The whole point of the WC22 table on the previous tab is that paper rank and result rank disagree, often by a lot.

Where to look for upsets. Apply the WC22 patterns: narrow depth gaps (deeper squads survive tournaments better); midfield-heavy ratings tend to overperform attack-heavy ones (Croatia 2018-2022 was the cleanest example); top-loaded squads underperform when their stars are tired or marked.

Method & sources

FIFA ratings are EA Sports' published national-team overall ratings from the relevant game version: FIFA 23 (released 30 Sep 2022) for WC22 and FIFA 26 (released 26 Sep 2025) for WC26. They're a weighted aggregate of the top-11 players' individual Overall ratings, with bonuses for synergy within positions. Depth gap is the average rating of the top-11 minus the average of squad members 12-23.

Tournament result rank: winner=1, RU=2, 3rd=3, 4th=4, QF losers ranked 5-8 by group-stage points, R16 losers 9-16, group-stage exits 17-32 by points then goal diff. Standard FIFA tournament ranking.

What FIFA Mode is good for. Identifying systematic over- and underperformers, framing the basketball-analogy question ("most talent on paper" vs "most likely to win"), and giving a shared baseline for fans who haven't watched every match. What it is not good for. Predicting the next match. Capturing chemistry (the whole point of the rest of the site). Anything that depends on the manager's tactical fit.
